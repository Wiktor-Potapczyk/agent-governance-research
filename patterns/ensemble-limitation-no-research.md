# Ensemble Lenses Reason But Don't Research

Multi-lens ensemble thinking (spawning parallel agents with different analytical perspectives) produces real divergence, but it is divergence between uninformed opinions. None of the lenses conduct research or verify claims against sources.

## The Principle

Ensemble produces HYPOTHESES, not conclusions. The winning insights need verification against actual evidence before they can drive decisions.

## Evidence

An ensemble analysis on a verification system design produced "kill confidence ratings" (Lens C) and "absorb into ensemble" (Lens D). A subsequent verification check against actual research showed: self-supervised reasoning uses confidence ratings and they work (+68% improvement). Neither lens checked.

Measured divergence across 4 lenses was 35% overlap — real divergence, but all four perspectives were ungrounded in external evidence.

## How to Apply

- After ensemble, the winning insights need verification against actual evidence (research, data, live system)
- The flow is: Ensemble (diverge) then Select (user picks) then Verify/Research (ground) then Build
- Never skip the grounding step just because 3/4 lenses agreed — agreement between uninformed perspectives is not evidence
- Consider whether a research step should precede ensemble for questions that depend on facts (not just framing)
