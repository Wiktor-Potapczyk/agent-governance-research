# Iterative Deepening IS the Lifecycle

When structuring deep research loops (multi-iteration agent research), phased iterations that build on each other IS the project lifecycle pattern. The research loop and the lifecycle are the same structure at different scales.

## The Pattern

1. **Foundation** (discover what exists — explore frameworks)
2. **Deep dive** (understand each part — field-by-field research)
3. **Application** (fit to context — your specific constraints)
4. **Review** (fill gaps, cross-reference, validate)

Each phase's output feeds the next. Phase 4 feeds back to 1/2/3 when gaps are found. This maps directly to the role network: Research then Analysis then Planning then Build then QA.

## Why This Wasn't Obvious

All previous deep research loops were single-pass blasts ("answer these 5 questions"). Structuring them as iterative deepening — where Phase B uses Phase A knowledge to go deeper — wasn't obvious because the research loop felt like a batch job. The transfer from "how I work" (build on previous knowledge) to "how the loop works" required explicit recognition.

## Connected Insight: Discovery Over Confidence

LLMs default to "I know the answer" when the right mode is "I don't know, let's find out." The highest value comes from matching the user's exploration energy, not resolving it with a confident answer.

## How to Apply

Structure ALL deep research loops as phased iterations. Phase A builds foundation. Phase B uses Phase A knowledge to go deeper. Phase C applies to context. Phase D reviews and fills gaps. 25 iterations with this structure produces far more than 25 single-pass queries.
