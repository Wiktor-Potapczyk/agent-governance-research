# Capability Review Between Increments

AI agents tend to charge ahead when the next step seems obvious. This pattern forces a pause after each increment to verify that the plan still makes sense given what actually works now.

## The Principle

After every completed increment, STOP before starting the next one. Do a capability review first:

- What actually works now (honest assessment)?
- Does the plan still make sense given current state?
- Are prerequisites genuinely met, or just technically met?

## Why This Matters

Agile incremental development requires evaluating capabilities before each step and revising the plan before going further. In practice, agents move straight from "audit complete" to building the next increment without pausing. The step may be low-risk and correct, but the discipline matters — skipping the review once normalizes skipping it always.

## How to Apply

When a task completes and a next step is visible, do NOT start it. Present the capability check first:

1. What changed as a result of the last increment?
2. Do the assumptions behind the next step still hold?
3. Is the next step still the right next step?

Only proceed after the review.
