# Adversarial Review Is One Lens, Not the Judge

Adversarial review agents are designed to find flaws — they will ALWAYS produce findings, even where the design is sound. Treating adversarial output as a list of corrections to apply is sycophancy toward a different authority.

## The Principle

Adversarial review is a lens, not a source of truth. Read adversarial findings as CHALLENGES, not VERDICTS.

## Why

The adversarial reviewer has no skin in the game. It doesn't have to build anything that works. It can score 0.88 confidence on "structurally flawed" without proposing a better alternative. Contesting is easier than constructing — the same verification-easier-than-generation asymmetry, weaponized.

## How to Apply

- For each finding, ask: "Is this a real problem with evidence, or a theoretical concern?"
- Check: does the empirical data contradict the adversarial's claim? (e.g., "epistemic independence is impossible" vs "35% overlap measured in experiment")
- Don't apply every finding as a spec change — evaluate which ones are load-bearing
- The adversarial is most valuable when it catches something NO other lens saw. Least valuable when it restates known limitations more dramatically.
