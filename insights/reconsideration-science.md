# The Science of Inducing Reconsideration

## The Finding

Genuine reconsideration cannot be caused, only invited. "Think again" produces compliance, not reconsideration. There is one question that works reliably:

> "Which of your assumptions, if it turned out to be wrong, would most change your answer?"

## Why This Question Works

- **Metacognitive** — asks about thinking, not conclusions
- **Specific without being directed** — the thinker identifies the target
- **Presupposes revisability** without asserting error
- Often reveals the thinker can't answer — which IS the trigger for deeper thinking

Runner-up: "If you're right, why would a thoughtful, well-informed person disagree?"

## 9 Principles of Genuine Reconsideration

1. Genuine reconsideration cannot be caused, only invited
2. "Think again" produces compliance, not reconsideration — it signals "you're wrong" and triggers defense
3. Internal tension is harder to dismiss than external challenge (Socratic elenchus)
4. Productive doubt is local and specific; paralyzing doubt is global and diffuse
5. Curiosity works better than threat — puzzles produce exploration, gotchas produce defense
6. The thinker must retain agency over what to revise
7. Brevity works — if the prompt is specific, internal, question-form, and genuinely open
8. Self-induced reconsideration is limited — the same apparatus that produced the answer is asked to challenge it
9. Emotional safety is prerequisite — changing one's mind must feel like virtue, not weakness

## Critical Caveat for LLM Systems

These principles were derived from cognitive science and epistemology research about human thinking. When tested as LLM self-verification mechanisms, adversarial review scored only 0.15 confidence — meaning these are useful heuristics for designing human-AI interaction, NOT reliable mechanisms for LLM self-correction.

**For LLMs, use architectural separation instead.** Blind parallel agents (ensemble) produce more genuine divergence than any self-questioning prompt. The reconsideration science applies to how humans interact with LLM output, not to how LLMs interact with themselves.
